## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM (Assessment A):** Not reflexively AI-generated — the procedural storm, lowercase eyebrows, and code-comment section labels are genuine brand-voice decisions. But it walks up to the line with category-default tells: the near-black + cyan/violet + JetBrains Mono + fake-terminal + lightning combo is *the* predictable "developer 2024–2026" costume, which slightly undercuts the "sob medida, sem templates" claim; gradient-word-per-heading rhythm; the 01–04 numbered scaffold (reskinned as `//` comments); an identical 3-card Lab grid; and a thin hero-metric pattern (3 "stat" cards dressing non-metrics on a studio with "1 cliente entregue").

**Deterministic (Assessment B) — detector exit 2, 4 findings:** `overused-font` (Space Grotesk — valid nudge, but a deliberate Space Grotesk + JetBrains Mono pairing); `single-font` (**false positive** — two families genuinely used); `em-dash-overuse` (21, mostly in code/terminal — low severity); `numbered-section-markers` (01–04 — advisory, deliberate here). `background-clip:text` was not flagged by this build; confirmed present on a few accent words as intentional brand identity.

**Where they agree:** the numbered markers and gradient-text are real patterns but load-bearing brand choices here, not accidental slop. The one hard false positive is `single-font`.

## What's Working

1. **The reactive procedural storm is real craft, not an asset drop.** Canvas particles + branching bolts whose charge tracks scroll velocity, with a `discharge()` climax on entering contact — bespoke, and directly serves the "page IS the proof" thesis. Scrim + vignette + section bg-alpha keep it as atmosphere.
2. **Unusually disciplined progressive enhancement.** `html.motion` gating, a full reduced-motion block, a genuine no-JS/CDN-blocked fallback, and focus-visible rings — robustness that itself signals engineering competence to the technical secondary audience.
3. **Copy voice defeats the template.** First-person, anti-template positioning ("feito à mão, sem templates", "você fala direto com quem constrói") reframes the solo studio as an advantage.

## Priority Issues

**[P1] Placeholder testimonial + dead WhatsApp links on a live trust page.** The proof section ships literal "Coloque aqui o depoimento… / — Nome do cliente, Empresa", and both `wa.me/5500000000000` links resolve to WhatsApp's invalid-number error. For a PME owner deciding in ~10s, visible placeholder copy reads "unfinished/template" and destroys trust; a dead WhatsApp kills the primary Brazilian contact channel. Fix: real mini-case or honest omission (no fake quote), and wire/remove WhatsApp. → **harden**

**[P1] Contact form is a no-feedback black-hole with no validation.** No `required`, no inline errors, empty submit fires a malformed mailto; on submit the page navigates to `mailto:` with no success state and does nothing on a client-less desktop. Highest-stakes interaction, zero reassurance/recovery. Fix: lightweight validation on name+message, a visible success state, and a non-mailto fallback (or make WhatsApp the primary submit). → **harden**

**[P2] `--text-faint` #5c6275 fails WCAG AA on all info-bearing small text.** Computed 3.2–3.34:1 (needs 4.5) on form labels (11.8px), hero-trust, hero-foot, `service-tags` (the tech stack — real info), `contact-or`, footer, placeholders. Over the storm-exposed hero/contact a bolt flash pushes it lower, and faint labels end up dimmer than their own placeholders (inverted hierarchy). Fix: lift faint info text to ≥ ~#8b91a8 (≈4.5:1); reserve sub-AA gray for truly decorative aria-hidden text only. → **clarify** / **colorize**

**[P2] Desktop process pins & traps 1.5× viewport for the least-critical content.** Confirmed pin distance 1230px = 1.50× viewport; inactive steps at `opacity:.36` (~2.5:1) are unreadable if paused. A ready-to-hire owner most wants the contact form; trapping them in "Do esboço ao deploy" reads as "the page won't move." Fix: shorten pin to ~0.8–1.0× viewport; raise inactive-step floor to `opacity:.6`. → **quieter** / **animate**

**[P3] Mobile has no section navigation.** `.nav-links` is `display:none` ≤860px with no hamburger replacement; only brand + a 39px (sub-44) "Iniciar projeto" remain. Wayfinding to Contato is lost on the devices most PME owners use. Fix: minimal mobile menu or anchored jump; bump nav button to ≥44px. → **adapt**

## Persona Red Flags

- **Jordan (first-timer):** the "O que você precisa" `<select>` has no chevron — looks like a filled text input, may not read as interactive; "Lab" is jargon; α/β/γ/δ read as cryptic or as placeholders.
- **Riley (stress tester):** empty submit fires a blank mailto; the `<select>` always pre-sends "Landing page"; both WhatsApp links dead; client-less mailto does nothing (button appears broken); clicking bare background fires a lightning bolt (easter-egg surprise).
- **Casey (mobile):** nav CTA 39px (<44px); no menu to reach contact; the desktop "proof" terminal is `display:none` <620px so the capability demo is absent on phones; 150-particle canvas risks jank on low-end Android; "feito no Brasil 🇧🇷" hidden on mobile.
- **PME owner (10-sec trust):** headline "Sites que fazem sua empresa parecer grande" is the best asset — a benefit hook straight to them. But seconds later: "1 cliente entregue" (thin) + the literal placeholder testimonial (reads unfinished), heavy jargon (RAG, multiagente, GraphQL, observabilidade) signals "too technical / not for me", and WhatsApp (their default channel) is broken. High-hook, leaky-trust.

## Minor Observations

- `og:image` missing (only twitter:card declared) → imageless social shares.
- `<select>` has no neutral default — always pre-selects "Landing page", mis-signaling intent.
- No skip-to-content link; no focus management after Lenis anchor scroll.
- Terminal is correctly `aria-hidden`, but it's also the primary desktop "proof" visual — invisible to AT users and all mobile users.
- Inactive-step `opacity:.36` over already-dim `--text-dim` is the worst legibility spot after the faint labels.
- Keep: discharge climax on contact, marquee pause-on-hover + aria-hidden, theme-color, reduced-motion storm at opacity .5.

## Questions to Consider

1. If "the page is the proof", why is the strongest proof — the terminal — hidden on phones, where most PME owners land? What's the mobile proof?
2. Is a fake-looking placeholder testimonial worse for trust than none? Would honest emptiness convert better?
3. Does "Do esboço ao deploy" earn 1.5 screens of trapped scroll, or is it what a ready-to-hire owner wants to skip?
4. For a WhatsApp-first PME audience, why is the primary CTA a mailto that silently fails on client-less desktops?
5. Is the storm concept enough to differentiate, or does the safe cyan/mono/terminal costume quietly contradict "sob medida, sem templates"?
